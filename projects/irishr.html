the challenge

What problem did we solve?

Traditional HR processes are heavily bottlenecked by manual administrative workflows, fragmented employee data, and inefficient payroll and performance tracking. Without a centralized system, managing varied access permissions for administrative staff, operational managers, and general employees leads to massive communication overhead, untracked data changes, and a slow, error-prone workforce lifecycle management process.

what we built

How did we solve it?

We developed IrisHR, a modern multi-tier HRMS web architecture designed to streamline operations via explicit, tailored user experiences. We mapped out clear operational workflows and implemented functional Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) across three primary levels—Admin, HR Manager, and Employee—ensuring that payroll processing, performance evaluations, and profile management are seamlessly partitioned, automated, and audited across the platform.

what i learned

What did this teach me?

Building a comprehensive HRMS forced me to transition from thinking about software as a collection of isolated features to viewing it as an interconnected operational ecosystem. Handling nuanced workplace dependencies like salary structures and sensitive personal identifiers taught me how critical data integrity and clear database modeling are to business operations. Architecting the RBAC system highlighted the complexity of software ergonomics, the massive technical gap between what information a user needs to see to perform their job efficiently versus what data boundaries must be strictly maintained at every endpoint. This project changed how I approach full-stack workflows, teaching me to map out user roles and business rules thoroughly before writing a single line of code.
