FocusPilot

An empathy-first interactive design prototype of a specialized focus and productivity application created to help students and young adults with ADHD manage daily tasks without feeling overwhelmed.

the challenge

What problem did we solve?

Standard productivity apps are often too rigid, text-heavy, or complicated for neurodiverse individuals. Users with executive dysfunction experience stress, guilt, and burnout from sudden notifications and unstructured task lists, the very tools meant to help them end up making things worse.

what we built

How did we solve it?

We designed a cross-platform interface that begins with a built-in clinical screening test to adaptively tune distraction-blocking features. The workspace features a visual circular focus timer, a favorites-enabled ambient sound deck, cross-device blocking synchronization, a task list prioritized by urgency, and a "Locked Mode" that prevents breaking focus until the current cycle finishes.

what i learned

What did this teach me?

FocusPilot was the first time I had to design for someone whose cognitive experience is fundamentally different from mine and that distinction forced a level of empathy I hadn't applied to design before. We did actual research into ADHD and executive dysfunction before touching Figma, and that changed everything. Features I would have considered "obvious" UX, like notification pop-ups and mandatory onboarding flows, turned out to be exactly what causes breakdowns for ADHD users. I learned that good design is about who you're actually designing for.
